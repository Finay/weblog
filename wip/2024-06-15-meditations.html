some thoughts after meditations

The first book I read in my gap year is Meditations by Marcus Aurelius. I can't fully deny reading it was a silly social media influenced attempt at becoming a stoic, but I was looking for a way to get a grasp on my life anyway. It's been a week since I finished the book and no, I do not have some new purpose and motivation by which I am living life. The book was never meant to teach that, the book is merely the notes of a long dead emperor attempting to live his life by a philosophy, not even meant to be published.
